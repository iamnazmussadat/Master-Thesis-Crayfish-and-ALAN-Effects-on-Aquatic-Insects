# A Mesocosm Experiment Assessing the Effects of Signal Crayfish and Artificial Light at Night on Aquatic Emergence Insects

This repository hosts the data, documentation, and overview of my Master of Science Thesis in Environmental Sciences, submitted to the Rhineland-Palatinate Technical University of Kaiserslautern-Landau (Campus Landau, Germany).

## 📌 Project Overview
Aquatic ecosystems are increasingly threatened by multiple concurrent anthropogenic stressors. This research investigates the individual and combined impacts of two prominent stressors on aquatic macroinvertebrates and their emergence patterns:
1. **Biological Invasion:** The invasive Signal Crayfish (*Pacifastacus leniusculus*).
2. **Sensory Pollution:** Artificial Light at Night (ALAN).

Using a controlled outdoor mesocosm setup, the study tracks how these stressors alter community structure, biomass, and the timing of aquatic insects emerging into terrestrial ecosystems—a critical energy subsidy pathway for terrestrial predators.

## 🔬 Experimental Methodology & Parameters
* **System Setup:** Controlled aquatic mesocosms mimicking natural stream/pond conditions.
* **Treatments Evaluated:** Control settings, isolated ALAN exposure, isolated Crayfish introduction, and combined (ALAN × Crayfish) cumulative interactions.
* **Key Indicators Measured:** Total insect abundance, taxonomical emergence composition, morphometric tracking, and biomass distribution over a multi-week monitoring timeline.

## 🛠️ Data Analysis Tools Used
* **Statistical Computing:** R / RStudio
* **Key Analysis Techniques:** Multivariate data modeling, community composition analysis, ANOVA tests, and ecological metrics calculations.
